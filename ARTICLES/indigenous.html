The Bicycle as a Divine Machine

For nearly two centuries, the bicycle has been a quietly efficient companion to humanity, often overshadowed by the dominance of oil-powered vehicles. Yet, as we confront the environmental and health consequences of our transportation choices, it's time to reevaluate the bicycle's significance in a new light. Conceived in a bygone era, the bicycle's design and perception were shaped by the limited understanding of its time. Today, with the benefit of hindsight and a deeper appreciation for sustainability, we can reexamine the bicycle's inherent value. Its unparalleled efficiency and direct transfer of human energy make it a testament to innovative design, and a potential key to a more sustainable future.
